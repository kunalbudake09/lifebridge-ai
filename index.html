Emergency CPR Chest Compression Guide

110 BPM Metronome

Chest compressions must be delivered at a rate of 100 to 120 compressions per minute (110 BPM) and to a depth of 2 inches (5 cm).

PUSH
Compressions: 00

CPR Quick Instructions:

  1. Ensure the scene is safe. Check if the patient responds by tapping their shoulder.
  2. If unresponsive, yell for help, dial 911/108, and start this metronome.
  3. Place the heel of one hand in the center of the chest and the other hand directly on top, interlocking fingers.
  4. Deliver firm, rapid compressions, pushing straight down matching each sound click and visual pulse.
  5. Allow full chest recoil between compressions. Do not lean on chest.
